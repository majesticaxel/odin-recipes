Basted Eggs

Description

One of our simpler recipes: basted eggs. As a deviation from our more sweeter recipes, we will provide something a little more salty. These eggs are a perfect breakfast and do not take long to make at all. With a combined prep and cook time of about 10 minutes, this recipe produces a delicious, simple egg with satisfying, set whites and a creamy, runny yolk. It's similar to a poached egg, but more flavorful and lessy fussy.

Ingredients

  • 1.5 tablespoons salted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 tablespoon water
  • 1/ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch flaky sea salt, or to taste

Steps

  1. Melt butter in a medium nonstick skillet over medium heat
  2. Carefully crack eggs into the skillet and let cook until whites have almost ser, about 1 minute.
  3. Add water, cover skillet with a lid or cookie sheet, reduce hear to medium-low, and cook until whites have set, yolks are still runny, and some of white has set over yolk, 1 to 2 more minutes.
  4. Remove lid and transfer eggs to a serving plate. Garnish with pepper and flaky salt, if desired. Serve immediately.
